5 Things Serious Players Need to Be Doing (and Probably Aren't)

1
Strengthen Your Hips, Glutes & Quads
The knee is a victim — not the culprit. Weak glutes and hips force your knee to absorb forces it was never designed to handle. Every cut, lunge, and split-step demands power from above. If your knee hurts, the fix is almost always upstream.
2
Train in All Three Planes of Motion
Pickleball doesn't happen in a straight line — and your training shouldn't either. You need lateral stability, rotational strength, and the ability to decelerate under control. Linear gym work alone won't protect your knees on the court.
3
Build Foot & Ankle Control
Every force you absorb travels up from the ground. Stiff or unstable ankles create a cascade of stress straight to your knee. Single-leg balance work, ankle mobility drills, and foot strengthening are non-negotiables for pickleball longevity.
4
Strength Train at Least Twice a Week
Tendons, cartilage, and muscles need progressive load to stay resilient. Two focused strength sessions per week is the minimum effective dose for players over 40. Skip it consistently and you slowly lose the structural support your knees depend on.
5
Recover Like It's Part of the Game
Sleep 7-9 hours. Hit 0.7-1g of protein per pound of bodyweight. Stay hydrated before, during, and after play. Take at least one full rest day per week. Recovery is when adaptation happens - without it, your knee never gets a chance to heal.
